## 2. Systemic Failures Identified

- **Llama 3.2 3B — full collapse of the structured schema (100% divergence, 39/39 responses).** The model emits syntactically valid JSON but returns `pontos_fortes` as a **list** instead of a **string**, violating the contract declared in the SYSTEM_PROMPT in 100% of cases under zero-shot regime. The bias is fully reversible under one-shot in-context demonstration; see the paper for the falsification protocol.
- **Phi-3 Mini — format deviations (100.0% divergence, 39/39 responses).** Despite being marketed by Microsoft as strong in structured reasoning, the model consistently fails when prompted in Brazilian Portuguese: omits keys, generates lists in place of strings, or returns free text inside the JSON. High variance across repetitions suggests low instruction stability in PT-BR.

## 3. AI Engineering Conclusion — Recommended Base Model

**Qwen 2.5 3B-Instruct** is the recommended base model for downstream pedagogical fine-tuning:
- **Classroom-compatible speed**: mean latency of **2906 ms** — ~5.1x faster than the slowest model tested and comfortably below the 10 s threshold for synchronous educational UX.
- **Full obedience to the JSON contract**: **0% divergence** (0/39). Emits `pontos_fortes` as string and `perguntas_reflexivas` as list exactly as the SYSTEM_PROMPT specifies — no grammar coercion needed at inference time.
- **Hardware compatible with public schools**: 3B parameters in Q4 quantization occupy ~2 GB RAM, feasible on modest laptops. The 8-9B models tested (Llama 3 8B, Gemma 2 9B) serve only as reference ceiling in the paper; they are not deployable for the 2026.2 pilot.
- **Headroom for pedagogical LoRA**: by selecting a model that already respects the schema, fine-tuning capacity can focus on the Koch metalinguistic rubric (theoretical focus, pedagogical distancing, Computational Thinking bridge) rather than spending adapter capacity fixing format — essential leverage given the still-small dataset (13 -> ~200-500 audited examples).

In summary: **Qwen 2.5 3B-Instruct** combines the best trade-off of speed, structural obedience, and hardware footprint among the 8 models evaluated, providing the most defensible infrastructure to sustain the next phase of fine-tuning on real student writing collected during the pilot.
